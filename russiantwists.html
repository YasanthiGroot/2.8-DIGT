Equipment needed:
1x dumbbell or medical ball

Muscles worked:
Core, oblique muscles

Instructions:
1. Sit on the floor or a mat with your knees bent and your feet flat on the ground
2. Lean back slightly, maintaining a straight spine and engaging your core muscles. Your torso should be at about a 45-degree angle to the ground.
3. Hold the weight with both hands in front of your chest.
4. Twist your torso to the right side, bringing the weight beside your right hip.
5. Bring the weight or your hands back to the centre of your body, maintaining control over the movement.
6. Twist your torso to the left side, bringing the weight beside your left hip.
7. Repeat the twisting motion from side to side for the desired number of repetitions.

Russian twists are a popular core-strengthening exercise that offers several benefits when incorporated into your fitness routine. Here are some of the key advantages of doing Russian twists:

Core Strength: Russian twists primarily target your core muscles, including the rectus abdominis (front of the abdomen) and the obliques (side of the abdomen). Regularly performing this exercise helps to strengthen these muscles, leading to improved core stability and overall strength. Oblique Engagement: The twisting motion of Russian twists specifically engages the oblique muscles, which are responsible for rotating and stabilising the torso. Strong obliques contribute to better posture, spinal alignment, and rotational movements. Functional Movement: Russian twists mimic twisting motions often used in daily activities and sports. Strengthening your oblique muscles through this exercise can enhance your ability to perform movements like reaching, bending, and twisting with greater control and stability. Improved Athletic Performance: Strong core muscles are essential for many athletic activities, such as running, cycling, golfing, and tennis. Engaging in exercises like Russian twists can lead to enhanced athletic performance by providing better core stability and power transfer. Balance and Coordination: Russian twists require balance and coordination as you rotate your torso while keeping your lower body stable. This can contribute to improved overall balance and coordination in various activities.

Incorporate Russian twists into a well-rounded core-strengthening routine alongside other exercises targeting different areas of the core. As with any exercise, focus on proper form and start with an appropriate level of intensity based on your fitness level.
